Parameter Definition
sAHI (SleepImage's Apnea Hypopnea Index equivalent) This is an automated measure of the frequency of Apnea and Hypopnea events per hour of sleep using a set of rules that include reduction of oxygen levels. The sAHI_3% uses as part of it detection of breathing events, a reduction of the oxygen by 3%. The sAHI_4% is a less sensitive measure and requires a 4% drop in the oxygen level. These are similar but not exactly the same, as the AHI used to report a patients breathing in the sleep lab.
Sleep Efficiency is the ratio of Total Sleep Time divided by Total Time in Bed. The ideal is > 85%.
TST_HOUR is the total sleep time in hours.
SpO2_Below90_Percent is the percentage of total sleep time where oxygen saturation drops below 90% during the sleep period.
SpO2_Below90_Duration_Min is the duration of total sleep time in Minutes where oxygen saturation drops below 90% during the sleep period.
